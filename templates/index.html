About

I am a Biostatistician in the Division of Epidemiology and Biostatistics, UCT. My current research interests include reproducible research, analysis of clinical trials, and computational and statistical methods for the analysis of recurrent events and compositional data.

My PhD looked at developing novel methods for the analysis of longitudinal microbiome sequencing data. These days I spend most of my free time trying to make data science as fast as possible by integrating Rust and R.

I am proficient in the usual data science tech stack: R, python, git, SQL, AWS. I also have extensive experience in the statistical analysis of longitudinal data and clinical trial data.

I am also a fan of simple, small, quick to load personal websites.
